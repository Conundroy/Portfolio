The rule to playing Werewolf is to trust no one. That tends to be the case before players would start lynching each other because that one player happened to be the Werewolf in the last session. Every social deduction game utimately devolves into senseless and uncalculating "lynching". Though fun, doesn't really challenge you.

Syndicate leans into that chaos and just hands you anarchy from the get go. Everyone is out for everyone in this Werewolf and Monopoly inspired boardgame of good versus evil... versus innocent bystanders. With 3 roles Mayor, Mob boss and Business owner, they must help each other in order to win, or ruin one another to get at another, giving much more dilemma to player's choices.

Raise taxes as the Mayor, raise interests as the Mob boss, commit tax evasion as a Business owner, and much more.

Old World Frontier
A post-apocalyptic RPG with business sim and RTS elements

A business sim survival horror inspired by the elusive Linkboys of 18th century Victorian era England, take on the role of a torch bearer in a time when street lights weren't common place, as you guide paying patrons home safely... or to their doom.

Take place right after the very first Industrial Revolution, mysterious black smoke engulfs the entire city in utter darkness. Life becomes a constant struggle between ensuring your own livelihood and people's safe travel home. This is when Linkboy encounters a mysterious cult, promising of a better life for its followers.

Torches need oil, and oil needs crafting, balance between your resources and finances. Guide people home under your light, or bring them to their final destination. After all, who else with a burning light are they going to run to?
