The rule to playing Werewolf is to trust no one. That tends to be the case before players would start lynching each other because that one player happened to be the Werewolf in the last session. Every social deduction game utimately devolves into senseless and uncalculating "lynching". Though fun, doesn't really challenge you.

Syndicate leans into that chaos and just hands you anarchy from the get go. Everyone is out for everyone in this Werewolf and Monopoly inspired boardgame of good versus evil... versus innocent bystanders. With 3 roles Mayor, Mob boss and Business owner, they must help each other in order to win, or ruin one another to get at another, giving much more dilemma to player's choices.

Raise taxes as the Mayor, raise interests as the Mob boss, commit tax evasion as a Business owner, and much more.
